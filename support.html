KillLine - Financial Risk Simulator

üì± About KillLine

KillLine is a financial risk simulator that uses Monte Carlo simulation to help you assess your financial resilience. Through hundreds or thousands of random simulations, it evaluates how well your finances can withstand unexpected events like job loss, medical emergencies, and other financial shocks.

üöÄ Getting Started

Step 1: Create Your Financial Profile

  1. Open the app and tap on the "Profile" tab
  2. Enter your monthly income and select income type
  3. Add your monthly expenses by category
  4. Enter your current emergency fund (savings)
  5. Choose your preferred currency

Step 2: Configure Simulation Settings

  1. Go to the "Settings" tab
  2. Choose simulation iterations (100, 1,000, or 10,000)
  3. Select a luck preset (Lucky, Normal, or Unlucky)
  4. Optionally customize individual event probabilities

Step 3: Run Your First Simulation

  1. Navigate to the "Dashboard" tab
  2. Tap "Run Simulation" button
  3. Watch the real-time animation (or skip to results)
  4. Review your financial health score and survival rate

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Monte Carlo simulation?

Monte Carlo simulation is a statistical technique that runs thousands of random scenarios to predict possible outcomes. In KillLine, it simulates your financial future by randomly generating risk events based on their probabilities, giving you a realistic picture of your financial resilience.

Q: How many simulations should I run?

100 iterations: Quick test (~1 second) - Good for initial exploration
1,000 iterations: Daily assessment (~5 seconds) - Recommended for regular use
10,000 iterations: Important decisions (~30 seconds) - Best accuracy for major financial decisions

Q: What do the luck presets mean?

Lucky üçÄ: All event probabilities reduced by 50% - For optimistic scenarios
Normal üòê: Based on statistical data - Realistic baseline
Unlucky üíÄ: All probabilities increased 2-3x - Stress testing worst-case scenarios

Q: Why is my health score low?

A low health score typically indicates insufficient emergency funds relative to your expenses. We recommend maintaining 6-12 months of expenses in your emergency fund. Review the recommendations tab for personalized suggestions to improve your score.

Q: Can multiple events happen at the same time?

Yes, multiple events can occur simultaneously in a simulation, except for unemployment and income reduction which are mutually exclusive. This reflects real-world scenarios where financial challenges often compound.

Q: How do I simulate the impact of buying a house?

Increase the housing expense probability to 10% and set the expense range to your expected down payment amount. This will help you assess whether you can handle the financial impact of a home purchase.

Q: Why does major illness affect both expenses and income?

Major illnesses typically require extended recovery time during which you cannot work normally, resulting in reduced income. The simulation reflects this reality by reducing income to 50% for 12 months in addition to medical expenses.

Q: Is my financial data safe?

Absolutely. All your data is stored locally on your device using Apple's Core Data framework. The app never connects to the internet, never uploads your data, and we have no servers. Your financial information stays completely private and under your control.

Understanding Results

Health Score

Your financial health score (0-100) indicates how well your finances can withstand unexpected events. It's calculated based on your survival rate across all simulations, median survival months, and worst-case scenarios.

Survival Rate

The percentage of simulations where you maintained positive reserves throughout the entire 60-month period. A higher survival rate indicates better financial resilience.

Median Survival Months

The middle value of how long you survived across all simulations. This gives you a realistic expectation of your financial endurance under various scenarios.

Best/Worst Case

Best Case: The highest ending reserve amount across all simulations (when luck is on your side)
Worst Case: The lowest ending reserve amount (when multiple unfortunate events occur)

Troubleshooting

App crashes or freezes during simulation

Try reducing the number of simulation iterations. If running 10,000 simulations, try 1,000 instead. Restart the app and try again. If the problem persists, please contact support.

Cannot save images to photo library

Go to Settings > Privacy > Photos and ensure KillLine has permission to add photos. You don't need to grant full photo library access, just "Add Photos Only" permission.

Data disappeared after app update

Your data should persist across updates. If you've lost data, check if you have an iCloud backup of your device. Restore from backup to recover your financial profiles and history.

Simulation results seem unrealistic

Check your event probability settings. If you've customized probabilities, they may be set too high or too low. Try resetting to a standard preset (Normal) and run the simulation again.

App not available in my language

Currently, KillLine supports English and Simplified Chinese. We're working on adding more languages in future updates. The app will automatically use your device's language if supported, otherwise it defaults to English.
